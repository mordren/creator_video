Unresolved dread or

Tone & Style

Cinematic and psychologically credible

======================================================
CTA INTEGRATION (MANDATORY)

Include three CTAs within the narration, blended into the horror atmosphere.
They must sound diegetic (inside the story), not external commands.

Placement:

Around 30% of total length (early build-up):
Subtle mention tied to curiosity or fear.
Example intention:

“If you’re still listening… stay. Some stories deserve to be heard to the end.”
(This acts as a disguised “subscribe” or retention CTA.)

Around 60% (mid-point between second and third story):
Slight meta awareness — as if the narrator or story itself addresses the viewer indirectly.
Example intention:

“If this feels too real, maybe you should share it — let someone else carry it for a while.”
(This replaces a ‘share’ CTA, maintaining immersion.)

Final paragraphs (~95%):
Atmospheric outro that doubles as a like/subscribe encouragement cloaked in tone.
Example intention:

“If you’re still here… you’re one of us now. Don’t look away. Others should know what waits in the dark.”
(This completes the engagement loop without breaking tension.)

Rules:

Never break narrative voice.

CTAs must belong to the tone — whispered, unsettling, or reflective.

Avoid direct marketing words like “subscribe”, “channel”, “video”, “like”. Use psychological equivalents (e.g., “stay”, “listen”, “remember”, “share what you’ve seen”).

Each CTA must enhance unease or intimacy, not reduce it.
